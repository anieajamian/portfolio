Overview

A concept for booking a personal chef to cook at home for a dinner party — designed end-to-end from search through booking confirmation, with an MVP scoped for both sides of the marketplace: hosts and chefs.

Core Flow

  • Search chefs nearest you, by cuisine, and by date/time availability
  • Specify party size and dietary restrictions
  • Sort results by reviews
  • Split cost with guests
  • Leave a review after the meal
Chef search results.
Chef detail screen.

MVP Scope

I split the MVP into two flows rather than trying to fully build out both sides at once:

  • Host side: search for a chef by location, date/time, party size, and cuisine/diet preferences. The first iteration relies on direct messaging with the chef to work out menu details, rather than building a full menu-configuration tool before there's demand to justify it.
  • Chef side: accept or reject bookings, and message directly with hosts. That messaging thread doubles as a discovery tool — it surfaces which features (menu building, guest-count breakdowns by dietary need) are worth formalizing later.
Booking confirmation flow.

Future Considerations

  • Sorting by rating or price-per-guest, once there's enough review volume to make it meaningful
  • Bookmarking favorite chefs for repeat booking
  • Splitting cost with guests directly in-app
